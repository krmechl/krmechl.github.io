Pons and Fleischmann got it right! Their approach to cold fusion works!


More than 30 years ago, Pons and Fleischmann postulated cold fusion heat
generation using an electrochemical process to trigger fusion at room
temperature — electrolysis in a heavy water bath with a palladium cathode. It
was a potential path to unlimited power production. The scientific community
was skeptical, and their process could never be replicated.


But that process actually does work, and we've got it functioning on the bench.
The process was not initially reproducible because Pons and Fleischmann were
scientists, and their design was flawed. And those who tried to duplicate it were
also scientists, following the flawed design.


Making it work turned out to be an engineering task. We are engineers — we've
made it work. Our RSCFusion Battery produces more continuous power than it
consumes on startup, using cold fusion to generate heat and electricity. Jump-
started with a 4500 mAh Li+ battery, it can run small kitchen appliances forever.

Overview

The Pons/Fleischmann Cell

The Pons/Fleischmann cold fusion device was intended to work by using
hydrogen and palladium to create a nuclear reaction at room temperature. The
concept was to have a container of heavy water electrolyte (deuterium) with a
palladium cathode and platinum anode. When electricity was applied to the
cathode/anode combination using a battery or other power source, heavy water
hydrogen molecules would migrate into the palladium cathode. Palladium has
an amazing ability to absorb hydrogen at tremendous volumes. It was believed
this large volume of hydrogen molecules would force individual molecules into
close proximity in the lattice structure within the metal. This atomic proximity
then would trigger the weak nuclear force, resulting in fusion at room
temperature. This would generate massive amounts of heat, along with helium
atoms and excess neutrons.


In fact, Pons and Fleishman did measure large heat production from this
process, on the order of 1,000 times what would be expected from normal
chemical reactions. They were not looking for and thus did not measure
neutron production. Others were not able to replicate these results

How It Works

The Pons/Fleischmann Concept

The energy released from the Pons/Fleischman reaction would be greater than the energy
required to initiate it, resulting in a net gain of energy. This process, had it worked as
expected, would have provided an almost limitless source of clean and cheap energy.


What we have done at RSCFusion is to update the design and add computer / software
controls to the electrolysis process. Our two main thrusts:

  • A better choice of metal for the anode
  • Microsecond real-time control of the current applied to the electrodes


Platinum does not work well as an anode in this situation (and is expensive), so we have
chosen a better alternative. And Pons /Fleischmann did not have access to modern,
powerful microchips 30 years ago. It turns out that precision software / hardware control of
the amount of electrical current being applied to the electrodes during startup makes
repeatable cold fusion possible.


Our bench-model RSCFusion Battery produces more power than that required to initiate
cold fusion. With a quart of heavy water, a 4500 mAh Li+ battery, a palladium cathode, and
our computer controls, one can start a fusion reaction in about 30 minutes, then generate
heat and electricity essentially forever.
